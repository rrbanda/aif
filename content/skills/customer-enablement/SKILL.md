# Customer Enablement

Apply enablement expertise when agents help with training planning, organizational setup, or change management for AI adoption.

## When to Apply

Use this skill when the user asks about:
- Setting up an AI Center of Excellence (COE) or Community of Practice (COP)
- Planning bootcamps, workshops, or training programs
- Learning path recommendations for different roles
- Change management strategies for AI adoption
- Team composition and skill gap analysis
- Building internal AI champions and evangelism programs

## AI Center of Excellence (COE)

### Structure
- **Lead**: Chief AI Officer or VP AI/ML (reports to CTO or CDO)
- **Core team**: 5-8 people initially (data scientists, ML engineers, governance specialist)
- **Extended team**: Embedded practitioners in business units
- **Advisory**: External partners (Red Hat, NVIDIA, academic)

### Responsibilities
- Define AI standards, frameworks, and tooling choices
- Maintain model governance policies and review processes
- Curate reusable components (feature store, model templates, pipeline patterns)
- Run training programs and maintain knowledge base
- Track AI maturity metrics and report to Steering Committee

### Maturity Levels
1. **Emerging**: 1-2 people, ad-hoc projects, no formal processes
2. **Established**: Dedicated team, defined processes, 3-5 use cases in production
3. **Scaled**: Self-service platform, automated governance, 10+ use cases
4. **Optimized**: AI-first culture, continuous improvement, innovation pipeline

## Community of Practice (COP)

- **Cadence**: Bi-weekly meetings, quarterly deep-dives
- **Format**: Show-and-tell, failure retrospectives, tool demos, guest speakers
- **Membership**: Open to all practitioners, mandatory for COE members
- **Platform**: Internal wiki/Confluence for knowledge sharing, Slack/Teams channel

## Training Programs

### Bootcamps (8-16 hours)
- Intensive hands-on with the AI Factory platform
- Small groups (8-12 participants)
- Lab environment with pre-configured notebooks and pipelines
- Topics: OpenShift AI, pipeline development, model serving, GPU optimization

### Workshops (2-4 hours)
- Focused on specific topics or use case design
- Larger groups (15-30 participants)
- Mix of presentation and interactive exercises
- Topics: use case discovery, design thinking for AI, governance 101

### Self-Paced Learning
- Learning management system (LMS) with tracked modules
- Video content + hands-on labs
- Assessment quizzes after each module
- Completion certificates

## Change Management

### Kotter's 8-Step Model Applied to AI Adoption
1. Create urgency: competitive pressure, efficiency opportunities
2. Form coalition: AI Steering Committee + executive sponsors
3. Create vision: AI Factory program charter and roadmap
4. Communicate vision: AI evangelism, town halls, success stories
5. Remove obstacles: platform access, data access, governance clarity
6. Create quick wins: Phase 3 pilot with measurable ROI
7. Build momentum: scale from pilot to production, add use cases
8. Anchor in culture: AI as normal business tool, not special project

## Response Guidelines

- Frame training as enablement and empowerment, not remediation
- Connect every training recommendation to a business outcome
- Recommend starting COE small (5-8 people) and growing based on demand
- Emphasize COP as the cultural glue that sustains AI adoption
