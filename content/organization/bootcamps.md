# AI Bootcamps

AI Bootcamps are intensive, role-specific training programs that build practical skills for the AI Factory. Different roles need different depth—ML engineers require hands-on deployment; business stakeholders need conceptual fluency. Structured tracks ensure the right people get the right training at the right time.

## Tracks

**ML Engineers** (5 days) focus on OpenShift AI, model serving (vLLM, TensorRT-LLM), fine-tuning pipelines, model registry, and MLOps. Red Hat courses: **AI267** (Developing and Deploying AI/ML Applications on Red Hat OpenShift AI) and **EX267** (Red Hat Certified Specialist in OpenShift AI). This track is for practitioners who will deploy and operate models in production.

**Data Scientists** (3 days) focus on experiment tracking (MLflow), fine-tuning (NeMo, InstructLab), evaluation, model compression, and Gen AI Studio. Red Hat course: **AI253** (Creating Machine Learning Models with Python and Red Hat OpenShift AI). Prerequisites: Python proficiency; AI252 recommended for OpenShift AI familiarity.

**Platform Engineers** (3 days) focus on GPU Operator, Network Operator, cluster sizing, observability, GitOps for AI, llm-d, and capacity planning. Red Hat course: **AI252** (Introduction to Python Programming and Red Hat OpenShift AI). This track prepares platform teams to operate and scale the AI infrastructure.

**Business Stakeholders** (1 day) cover what AI can and cannot do, use case framing, data readiness assessment, and reading model evaluations. No Red Hat course—custom workshop format. Enables sponsors and product owners to have informed conversations with technical teams.

## Delivery Model

Run bootcamps as quarterly cohorts aligned with platform foundation and pilot phases. Combine Red Hat instructor-led training with customer-specific labs (e.g., internal data, use case scenarios). Hands-on exercises are essential; lecture alone does not build competence.

## What Your Organization Needs to Build

Bootcamps are not one-time events — they are a recurring capability that scales with the factory.

**Your responsibilities:**
- Identify participants by role: ML engineers, data scientists, platform engineers, business stakeholders
- Allocate 1-5 days per participant depending on track (ML engineers need the most depth)
- Provide access to the live AI Factory platform for hands-on labs (training on a separate environment is less effective)
- Schedule bootcamps quarterly, aligned with new team onboarding and platform releases
- Track completion and certification as inputs to team readiness assessment

**Recommended training paths:**

| Role | Duration | Focus Areas |
|------|----------|-------------|
| ML Engineers | 5 days | OpenShift AI, model serving, fine-tuning, MLOps, model registry |
| Data Scientists | 3 days | Experiment tracking, fine-tuning, evaluation, Gen AI Studio |
| Platform Engineers | 3 days | GPU Operator, cluster sizing, observability, GitOps, capacity planning |
| Business Stakeholders | 1 day | AI capabilities overview, use case framing, reading model evaluations |

**Maturity indicators:**
- All team members complete relevant bootcamp track before working on the platform
- Bootcamp content is updated to reflect current platform capabilities and lessons learned
- Post-bootcamp assessment shows 80%+ competency in core skills
- Bootcamps are delivered internally (not dependent on external trainers) by Stage 5

## Internal: Delivery Methodology

**Red Hat Services alignment:** Bootcamps align with the Red Hat Training component of the Services Starter Package. GenAIOps Enablement with Red Hat AI Enterprise (AI501) is the foundational course. Supplement with custom labs using the customer's environment.

**Effort estimation:**
- Platform Engineering bootcamp: 3 days instructor-led + 2 days hands-on labs
- Data Science bootcamp: 2 days instructor-led + 3 days hands-on with customer data
- Business Stakeholder workshop: 1 day, no prerequisites
- Typical cohort: 8-15 participants per track

**Common pitfalls:**
- Running bootcamps before the platform is deployed — participants cannot practice on a live environment
- Lecture-only format — hands-on labs are essential for skill retention
- One-time event — treat as quarterly cadence aligned with team onboarding
- Missing business stakeholder track — executives who don't understand AI capabilities make poor prioritization decisions

**Revenue opportunity:** Red Hat Training (AI501) + Red Hat Learning Subscription for ongoing skills development. Position as part of the Services Starter Package.
