# AI Evangelism

AI Evangelism is the internal advocacy program that makes AI Factory successes visible, drives adoption, and builds organizational momentum. Technical capability alone does not scale—people need to see what works, understand how to participate, and feel that the program is real and progressing.

## Demo Days

Demo days showcase working use cases to a broader audience. Invite stakeholders beyond the immediate delivery teams: business leaders, adjacent functions, and skeptics. Live demos with real data (or realistic synthetic data) are more persuasive than slides. Schedule quarterly; align with steering committee or all-hands cycles.

## Executive Briefings

Quarterly executive briefings update leadership on factory value, roadmap, and risks. Use value realization metrics: models in production, business impact, adoption growth. Keep slides concise; executives want outcomes, not implementation detail. These briefings reinforce sponsorship and secure continued investment.

## Internal Newsletter or Blog

A regular internal newsletter or blog shares success stories, new capabilities, team spotlights, and practical tips. It reaches people who do not attend demos or CoP sessions. Include links to documentation, training, and how to get started. Frequency: bi-weekly or monthly.

## Adoption Metrics Dashboard

A visible dashboard tracks adoption: teams using the factory, API calls, deployments, and satisfaction scores. Make it accessible to practitioners and leadership. Transparency creates accountability and highlights gaps (e.g., underused regions or functions).

## Cross-Team Matchmaking

Connect teams with similar challenges or complementary capabilities. A team struggling with model serving may benefit from another team's experience; a team with excess GPU capacity may share with one that is constrained. Evangelism includes facilitating these connections.

Evangelism is often under-resourced. Recommend assigning a part-time evangelist (internal or Red Hat) early. Content can be co-created: Red Hat provides templates and examples; the customer adds local context and stories.
