# Executive Summary

## The Challenge

Financial services organizations face a critical gap between AI experimentation and production-scale deployment. Individual teams build models in isolation, data governance varies across business units, and GPU infrastructure sits underutilized. The result: AI remains a cost center generating proofs-of-concept rather than a capability driving measurable business outcomes.

## The AI Factory Approach

The AI Factory is a structured program that builds the organizational capability to continuously produce, deploy, and operate AI. It combines Red Hat OpenShift AI and NVIDIA AI Enterprise with consulting services to establish:

- **A unified AI platform** on the organization's existing OpenShift infrastructure, eliminating shadow AI deployments and enabling centralized governance
- **Reusable pipelines** for data preparation, model training, evaluation, and deployment that any team can use
- **Governance frameworks** meeting PCI DSS, GDPR, and SOX requirements with automated compliance gates
- **Organizational structures** — Steering Committee, Center of Excellence, and Community of Practice — that sustain the factory beyond the initial engagement

## Expected Outcomes

By the end of the initial program (approximately 12 months):

- 2-3 production AI use cases generating measurable business value
- Self-service model development and deployment capability for multiple teams
- GPU infrastructure operating at >70% utilization
- Automated governance gates for every model deployment
- Internal AI skills across ML engineering, data science, and platform engineering roles

## Investment Timeline

The program progresses through gated phases. Each phase concludes with a value gate — measurable criteria that must be met before proceeding. This prevents over-investment before proving value and gives executive leadership clear decision points.
