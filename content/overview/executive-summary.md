# Executive Summary

## The Problem

88% of AI pilots fail to reach production (IDC/Lenovo 2024). The pattern is consistent across industries: individual teams build models in isolation, data governance varies across business units, GPU infrastructure sits underutilized or unprovisioned, and AI remains a cost center generating proofs-of-concept rather than a capability driving measurable business outcomes.

This is not a technology gap. The models work. The gap is organizational — no standardized pipelines, no governance framework, no repeatable process for moving AI from experiment to production. Every project is a one-off, and each one-off has a high probability of failure.

Meanwhile, AI infrastructure is the largest technology buildout in history. The cost of AI inference falls roughly 10x every 12 months. Organizations that build the factory now capture this cost curve. Organizations that wait will buy it from competitors.

## The AI Factory

The AI Factory is a co-engineered platform from Red Hat and NVIDIA that establishes the organizational capability to continuously build, deploy, and operate AI at scale. It combines Red Hat OpenShift AI and NVIDIA AI Enterprise with the processes, governance, and people structures required to make production AI repeatable.

The factory produces four categories of output:

- **Predictions** — real-time scoring, fraud detection, risk assessment, demand forecasting, clinical risk stratification
- **Pattern Recognition** — anomaly detection, regulatory change detection, equipment degradation, disease markers
- **Process Automation** — compliance checking, document routing, clinical documentation, predictive maintenance scheduling
- **Agentic AI** — autonomous agents that reason, plan, and act using enterprise tools and data with governance guardrails

## Expected Outcomes

Within approximately 12 months of initial deployment:

| Outcome | Target |
|---------|--------|
| Production AI use cases generating measurable business value | 2-3 |
| Self-service model development and deployment across teams | Operational |
| GPU infrastructure utilization | >70% |
| Governance gates for every model deployment | Automated |
| Internal AI skills (ML engineering, data science, platform engineering) | Established |

## How It Works

The factory matures through seven stages — from discovery through continuous operations. Each stage builds on the previous, with value gates ensuring measurable progress before advancing. Two parallel tracks run simultaneously: the **Technical Track** (platform, data, models, operations) and the **Organizational Track** (governance, expertise, skills, culture, change management).

The platform is built on a five-layer architecture co-engineered by Red Hat and NVIDIA: infrastructure, accelerated compute, AI platform, foundation models, and intelligent applications. All components run on-premises. No data leaves the environment.

## Investment Approach

The factory matures through gated stages. Each stage concludes with a value gate — measurable criteria that must be met before proceeding. This prevents over-investment before proving value and gives executive leadership clear decision points at every step.

The initial investment (Stages 0-2) establishes the platform and proves feasibility. Stages 3-4 deliver production use cases and operationalize the pipeline. Stage 5 scales across the organization. Stage 6 transitions to continuous operations with ongoing partnership and support.
